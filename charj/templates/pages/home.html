FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do credit card companies close inactive accounts?
Credit card issuers close inactive accounts to reduce risk and administrative costs. Typically, accounts with no activity for 12-24 months are at risk of closure, which can negatively impact your credit score by reducing your available credit and average account age.
How does Charj keep my credit cards active?
Charj automatically charges a small amount to each credit card you add on a schedule you choose. You pick the amount and frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly). This regular activity keeps the card active in the issuer's system, preventing closure due to inactivity while maintaining your credit history and score.
Is Charj secure and PCI compliant?
Yes, Charj is powered by Stripe, an industry-leading payment processor. We never store your credit card information. All card data is securely handled by Stripe, which is PCI DSS Level 1 certified - the highest level of security certification in the payments industry.
Can I cancel my subscription at any time?
Yes, you can cancel any or all of your card subscriptions at any time through your dashboard. There are no contracts, cancellation fees, or hidden charges. You only pay for the cards you want to keep active at the rate you choose.
How much does Charj cost?
You choose your own pricing! Select any amount starting from $0.50 and pick your billing frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly). There are no setup fees or hidden charges. You only pay for the cards you add to the service at the rate you select.
What happens if a payment fails?
If an annual charge fails (e.g., due to an expired card or insufficient funds), we'll notify you immediately via email. You can update your payment method through your dashboard. We'll retry the payment automatically, and your card will remain in our system.
How many credit cards can I add?
There's no limit! You can add as many credit cards as you want to keep active. Each card gets its own subscription with the amount and frequency you choose.
Will these small charges affect my credit score?
No, small recurring charges will not negatively impact your credit score. In fact, they help by keeping your cards active and maintaining your credit history. The positive impact of preserving account age and available credit far outweighs any minimal effect from the charges.
