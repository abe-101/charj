Frequently Asked Questions

Why do credit card companies close inactive accounts?

Credit card issuers close inactive accounts to reduce risk and administrative costs. Typically, accounts with no activity for 12-24 months are at risk of closure, which can negatively impact your credit score by reducing your available credit and average account age.

How does Charj keep my credit cards active?

Charj automatically charges $1 per year to each credit card you add to our service. This small annual charge keeps the card active in the issuer's system, preventing closure due to inactivity while maintaining your credit history and score.

Is Charj secure and PCI compliant?

Yes, Charj is powered by Stripe, an industry-leading payment processor. We never store your credit card information. All card data is securely handled by Stripe, which is PCI DSS Level 1 certified - the highest level of security certification in the payments industry.

Can I cancel my subscription at any time?

Yes, you can cancel any or all of your card subscriptions at any time through your dashboard. There are no contracts, cancellation fees, or hidden charges. You only pay $1 per card per year for the cards you want to keep active.

How much does Charj cost?

Charj costs exactly $1 per credit card per year. There are no setup fees, monthly fees, or hidden charges. You only pay for the cards you add to the service, and charges are made annually.

What happens if a payment fails?

If an annual charge fails (e.g., due to an expired card or insufficient funds), we'll notify you immediately via email. You can update your payment method through your dashboard. We'll retry the payment automatically, and your card will remain in our system.

How many credit cards can I add?

There's no limit! You can add as many credit cards as you want to keep active. Each card is charged separately at $1 per year.

Will the $1 charge affect my credit score?

No, a small $1 annual charge will not negatively impact your credit score. In fact, it helps by keeping your cards active and maintaining your credit history. The positive impact of preserving account age and available credit far outweighs any minimal effect from the charge itself.
